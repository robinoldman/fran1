Francis Wade

Francis Wade is a writer and editor based in London. His work focuses on issues related to political upheaval and violence, migration and borders, and climate.

He publishes essays and book reviews, as well as interviews, in the London Review of Books, The Guardian, The Nation, Washington Post, The Baffler, New York Review of Books and elsewhere. His book, Myanmar’s Enemy Within: Buddhist Violence and the Making of a Muslim ‘Other’ (Bloomsbury/Zed Books), first published in 2017, was based on his years-long investigations into the processes that culminated in genocidal violence towards Rohingya in Myanmar in 2017, and its relationship to the period of liberalization there. It went to a second edition in 2019.

Between 2009 and 2016, he reported from across South and Southeast Asia on democratization and military coups, conflict and large-scale displacement, for US and European magazines and newspapers. In addition, he has written on the impact of violent rhetoric on UK politics, the psychology of climate protest movements, the colonial origins of postcolonial violence, the effect of the climate crisis on citizenship and legal status, and the failures of the UN in contexts of ethnic cleansing and genocide.
